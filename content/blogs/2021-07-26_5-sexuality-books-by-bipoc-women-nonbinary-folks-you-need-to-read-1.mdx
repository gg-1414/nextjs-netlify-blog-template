    “Hope is a woman who has lost her fear.” -  Alice Walker


    The Color Purple, by Alice Walker, explores race, feminism and queer love and should be on everyone’s reading list.  If you’re anything like me, you’ve read this book cover to cover more than a couple of times. Lucky for you, I have created a reading list that explores similar themes around sexuality, written exclusively from the point of view of feminist writers of color. Here are 5 Books on Sexuality all written by women of color:
  - >-
    ![Pleasure Activism by Adrienne Maree
    Brown](/images/ec_varuna_sexuality_books_1.jpg "Pleasure Activism by
    Adrienne Maree Brown")


    ### Pleasure Activism, adrienne maree brown


    A NYT bestseller that explores the politics of feeling good. In their book, adrienne brings in Black feminist teachings to touch upon social justice and healing as resistance. According to the author, many people who don’t fit the norm of society are denied pleasure and how prioritizing their sexual wellness is central to our activism. This book highlights sex work, climate change, race and gender to sex and drugs. You can follow adrienne’s work [here](https://www.instagram.com/adriennemareebrown/?hl=en) and buy their book [here](https://www.akpress.org/pleasure-activism.html).
  - >-
    ![What a time to be alone by Chidera
    Eggerue](/images/ec_varuna_sexuality_books_2.jpg "What a time to be alone by
    Chidera Eggerue")


    ### What a time to be alone, Chidera Eggerue


    You might know internet sensation, Chidera Eggerue by their pseudonym ‘Slumflower’, but what you didn’t know is that they are the founder of #saggyboobsmatter and the author of this incredible book on exploring single life. Much of society’s emphasis is around coupling up and being sexual within the confines of a romantic relationship. However, in their book, Chidera brings in proverbial teachings from their Nigerian heritage to highlight the importance of prioritizing the self and platonic relationships. You can follow Chidera’s work [here](https://www.instagram.com/theslumflower/?hl=en) and buy their book [here](https://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/what-a-time-to-be-alone-chidera-eggerue/1128305625).

    ### Bodymap, Leah Lakshmi Piepzna-Samarasinha


    A must read for every poetry fan. Leah Lakshmi Piepzna-Samarasinha is a Sri Lankan queer disabled femme-of-colour. As one of the finalist for the Finalist, Audre Lorde Award in 2016, Leah Lakshmi explores themes of sex, lesbian love and disability. Bodymap introduces a profoundly refreshing narrative around  intersectional feminism. You can follow Leah’s work [here](https://www.instagram.com/leahlakshmiwrites/?hl=en) and buy their book [here](https://www.mawenzihouse.com/product/bodymap/).

    ### Body is Not an Apology, Sonya Renee Taylor


    Another NYT bestseller written by Black feminist Sonya Renee Taylor in which they celebrate the body and all it’s changes. The book is a result of a radical social justice movement that centers on that very theme, a digital community founded in 2011. Through their online platform and book, Sonya is a thought leader on self love, body empowerment, and global intersectionality. You can follow Sonya’s work [here](https://www.sonyareneetaylor.com/) and buy their book [here](https://www.sonyareneetaylor.com/books/the-body-is-not-an-apology-the-power-of-radical-self-love-h2x6p).

    ### We Have Always Been Here: A Queer Muslim Memoir, Samra Habib


    Winner of the 2020 Lambda Literary award, Samra writes about their personal journey of marriage, abuse, racism and queer sexuality. In this memoir, she talks of growing up in a muslim refugee family in Canada, and how she escaped an abusive arranged marriage. Samra bares it all, highlighting cultural influences on sexuality and how coming out was not just an act of rebellion but a way for them to find their truest self. You can follow Samra’s work [here](https://www.instagram.com/samra.habib/?hl=en) and buy their book [here](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/565780/we-have-always-been-here-by-samra-habib/).
